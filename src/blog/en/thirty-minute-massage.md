Thirty minutes can be enough if the goal is very specific. For example, working on neck and shoulders, one area of the back, or a localized discomfort can fit into a short session.

## When it can work

A 30-minute session can be useful for maintenance, follow-up, or targeted relief. It can also help when someone has little time and wants to focus on one clear priority.

## When it may be too short

If there are several tense areas, chronic pain, high physical stress, or a need for detailed assessment, 30 minutes can limit the work. In those cases, 60 or 90 minutes allow a more complete treatment.

## The key is choosing well

A short session needs a clear goal. If the whole body is treated in very little time, the result can feel superficial.
