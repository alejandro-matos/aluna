Manual lymphatic drainage is a gentle technique that supports fluid movement in the body. It is often used when there is heaviness, swelling, fluid retention, or during certain post-surgical recovery processes.

## Why it is different

It does not use deep pressure. The movements are slow, rhythmic, and precise because the lymphatic system responds best to soft, consistent stimulation.

## Common situations

It can be useful after aesthetic or surgical procedures, once a healthcare professional has approved manual treatment. It may also help with congestion, body fatigue, or mild swelling.

## Important

Lymphatic drainage does not replace medical evaluation. If there is intense pain, fever, marked redness, infection, or suspicion of thrombosis, consult a healthcare professional first.
