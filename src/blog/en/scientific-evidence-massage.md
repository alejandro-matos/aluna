Research on massage shows benefits in areas such as musculoskeletal pain, stress, anxiety, wellbeing, and functional recovery. Evidence does not mean massage cures everything; it means it can be a useful tool within an appropriate plan.

## Where it often helps

Studies often find improvements in pain, muscle tension, relaxation, quality of life, and perceived wellbeing. Many people also report better mobility and less stiffness after well-focused sessions.

## It depends on the case

Effectiveness changes according to the condition, technique, frequency, therapist experience, and the person's needs. That is why treatment should be adapted instead of always applying the same routine.

## A complementary tool

Therapeutic massage can complement exercise, rest, body education, and medical follow-up when needed. Its value is in being used with judgment, not as a single solution.
