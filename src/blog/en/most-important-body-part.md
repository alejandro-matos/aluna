The most important body part to massage depends on the person, the goal of the session, and what is causing the discomfort. Sometimes pain appears in one place, but the main tension comes from somewhere else.

## It is not always where it hurts

Neck pain can relate to shoulders, upper back, jaw, or even breathing. Low back discomfort may be influenced by hips, glutes, or legs. That is why a good session starts with listening and assessment.

## Areas that are often key

Back, neck, shoulders, hips, and legs often carry a lot of daily load. But more important than choosing a famous area is understanding how the whole body is moving.

## The goal sets the priority

If you are looking for mobility, recovery, or pain relief, the most important area is the one that contributes most to that goal. In manual therapy, judgment matters more than a fixed routine.
